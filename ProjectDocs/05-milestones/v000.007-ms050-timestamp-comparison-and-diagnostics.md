## Behavior Specification

### Inputs

- ``normalized metadata containing zero or more timestamp representations (naive, timezone-aware, UTC)``
- ``timestamp candidates originating from different sources``

### Processing Rules

- ``comparison must occur only between directly comparable timestamp representations``
- ``UTC timestamps are comparable with UTC timestamps``
- ``timezone-aware timestamps are comparable after conversion to UTC``
- ``naive timestamps are comparable only with naive timestamps``
- ``naive timestamps must not be compared with timezone-aware or UTC timestamps``
- ``timezone-aware timestamps may be converted to UTC only if offset is explicit and valid``
- ``no implicit timezone inference is allowed``
- ``comparison must not modify selected timestamp (MS048)``
- ``comparison must not introduce precedence or fallback behavior``
- ``inconsistency must be detected when two comparable timestamps differ in value``
- ``non-comparable timestamps must be ignored``
- ``diagnostics must be produced deterministically from compared data only``
- ``diagnostics must not affect processing outcome``

### Outputs

- ``comparison results for comparable timestamp pairs``
- ``inconsistency flags for differing comparable timestamps``
- ``deterministic diagnostics structure describing comparisons``
- ``no modification of selected timestamp or pipeline output``

### Edge Cases

- ``fewer than two comparable timestamps → no inconsistency``
- ``only non-comparable timestamps → no comparison``
- ``invalid timezone offset → no conversion``
- ``identical comparable timestamps → no inconsistency``
- ``multiple differing comparable timestamps → inconsistency detected deterministically``
- ``no timestamps → valid empty diagnostics output``

Rules:

- Must be explicit and complete
- Must be independently understandable
- Must not rely on implicit behavior
- Must not depend on environment-specific conditions
